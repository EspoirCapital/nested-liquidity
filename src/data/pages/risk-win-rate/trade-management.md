      <div class="page-kicker">05 — Risk & Win Rate</div>
      <h1>Trade Management</h1>
      <p class="lede">Designed as a low-RR system as a mechanical baseline. Discretion sharpens it further.</p>
      <div class="stat-grid">
        <div class="stat-card"><div class="v">1 – 1.5</div><div class="l">Target RR</div></div>
        <div class="stat-card"><div class="v">67 – 70%</div><div class="l">Mechanical Win Rate</div></div>
        <div class="stat-card"><div class="v">~80%</div><div class="l">With Discretion Applied</div></div>
      </div>
      <p>Use a market order for entry exactly at the close of the candle that inversed the iFVG. You can use a limit order for a better risk to reward but you risk missing out on setups.</p>
      <div class="def-box">
        <div class="db-label">Stop Loss Placement</div>
        <p>Place your initial stop loss at the high or low created by the sweep itself — the wick that took out the NH/NL.</p>
      </div>
      <p>Set your SL and TP at the same time when entering the trade — don't wait to decide on an exit later.</p>
      <p>Only move your stop loss to half risk once price has moved at least half of the target (0.5RR) in your favor — don't move it earlier.</p>
      <div class="callout warn">
        <strong>Never breakeven —</strong> price will typically retest the entry before continuing to TP, so moving to full breakeven risks getting stopped out on that retest.
      </div>
      <p>The following pages cover each discretion filter that can be layered on top of the mechanical rules to raise win rate toward the upper end of that range.</p>
