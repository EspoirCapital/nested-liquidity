      <h1>Propfirms.</h1>
      <p class="lede">Funding your trading without risking your own savings — how prop firm challenges work, explained simply.</p>

      <h2>What is a prop firm?</h2>
      <p>A proprietary trading firm lets you trade a <strong>simulated funded account</strong> (demo environment) after passing an evaluation. You pay a one-time challenge fee (typically $80–$600 depending on account size), pass their rules, and you get access to what's essentially a large demo account with a profit target. If you hit the target and follow the rules, they pay you real money — treating the simulated profits as if they were real.</p>
      <p>It's misleading to say you're "trading the firm's capital." In reality, all trading happens in a demo environment. The firm pays you from the challenge fees collected from all traders. That said, it's still a good deal: instead of depositing $50k of your own money, you pay a few hundred dollars for the chance to earn real payouts on simulated profits. Examples: FTMO, FundedNext, FundingPips, The5ers.</p>

      <h2>How evaluations work</h2>
      <p>Most top firms have moved to <strong>no time limits</strong>. You can take as long as you need to hit the profit target. This is the biggest change in 2025–2026 — the pressure is off.</p>
      <p><strong>1-Step Evaluation (FTMO, TopStep).</strong> Single phase. Hit the profit target (10% on FTMO) while respecting the daily loss and max drawdown limits. Pass and you're funded. No verification phase. FTMO requires 0 minimum trading days and no consistency rule for standard accounts.</p>
      <p><strong>2-Step Evaluation (FundedNext, most others).</strong> Phase 1 (Evaluation): hit 8–10% profit target. Phase 2 (Verification): hit 4–5% to prove it wasn't luck. Then funded. Most require 4–5 minimum trading days per phase. No time limit on either phase.</p>

      <h2>Key rules explained simply</h2>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Profit Target.</strong> How much you need to make. On a $100k account with a 10% target, you need $10,000 in profit. No time limit means you can take months.</li>
        <li><strong>Daily Loss Limit.</strong> How much you can lose in a single day. Industry standard is 5% of your account. On $100k that's $5,000 max loss per day. If you hit this, the challenge is over. Some firms (FTMO new model) use 3%.</li>
        <li><strong>Max Drawdown.</strong> How much you can lose total from your peak. Two types:
          <br><em>Static Drawdown</em> (FundedNext, The5ers) — your floor is fixed at 90% of the starting balance. If you make $20k profit, your floor stays at $90k, giving you more room.
          <br><em>Trailing Drawdown</em> (FTMO new, Topstep) — your floor moves up with your equity peak. If you reach $110k, your floor becomes $100k. Protects the firm from losing their profits.</li>
        <li><strong>Consistency Rule.</strong> Some firms cap how much one day can contribute to total profit. FundedNext: best day ≤ 40% of total. The5ers: best day ≤ 50%. Prevents passing on one lucky trade.</li>
        <li><strong>Minimum Trading Days.</strong> Some firms require a minimum number of days with at least one opened and closed trade. FTMO (new): 0. FundedNext: 5 per phase. TopStep: 0.</li>
        <li><strong>News Trading.</strong> Most firms now allow news trading, though some restrict high-impact events (NFP, FOMC) on certain account types.</li>
      </ul>

      <h2>Scaling</h2>
      <p>Once funded and profitable, most firms offer a scaling plan. Hit consistent monthly targets and your account size increases — $50k becomes $75k, then $100k, etc.</p>
