      <h1>Swing High &amp; Swing Low</h1>
      <div class="def-box">
        <div class="db-label">Definition</div>
        <p><strong>Swing High:</strong> A 3-candle pattern where the middle candle has the highest high — the candles before and after have lower highs. Price peaked and reversed.</p>
        <p style="margin-top:12px;"><strong>Swing Low:</strong> A 3-candle pattern where the middle candle has the lowest low — the candles before and after have higher lows. Price bottomed and reversed.</p>
      </div>
      <p class="lede">These become <strong>Nested Highs</strong> and <strong>Nested Lows</strong> when they sit inside an FVG — the core setup of this strategy.</p>
      <div class="page-img">
        <img src="/images/swing-high-swing-low-example.png" alt="Swing High and Swing Low examples on a chart">
      </div>
      <p class="img-desc">Multiple swing highs and swing lows — each is a 3-candle formation where the middle candle's high or low stands above or below its neighbors, marking a turning point where price reversed</p>
      <p>The swing point gives you the level to watch. The FVG tells you it hasn't been fully traded through yet. Both are needed — the swing point marks <em>where</em> liquidity sits, and the FVG marks <em>why</em> it's still there.</p>
