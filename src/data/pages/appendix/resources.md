      <h2 style="margin-top:0;">Charting &amp; Trading Platforms</h2>
      <p><strong>TradingView</strong> — the free charting platform used by most ICT-style traders. Pine Script support makes it possible to build custom indicators like the Nested Liquidity detector. Set up a multi-timeframe layout with M15/M30 on the main chart and M2/M3 in a separate pane below.</p>
      <p><strong>MatchTrader</strong> and <strong>MetaTrader 5 (MT5)</strong> — two widely used execution platforms with prop firms. <strong>MatchTrader</strong> is browser-based (no install needed) and includes an integrated risk management calculator. <strong>MT5</strong> is a long-standing industry standard with more advanced order management but a steeper learning curve.</p>

      <h2>Propfirms vs. Brokers</h2>
      <p>For retail traders starting out, <strong>prop firms</strong> are often a better option than opening a personal broker account. Instead of depositing your own $50k, you pay a challenge fee (a few hundred dollars) to trade a simulated funded account. Pass the rules and you earn real payouts on simulated profits — same opportunity without needing to save up years of capital.</p>
      <p>Standard brokers require significant personal capital to make meaningful returns. The catch with prop firms: all trading is on demo, drawdown rules are strict, and you're trading on a leash. For traders with limited capital, prop firms provide a way to access large simulated accounts at a fraction of the cost of depositing your own funds.</p>

      <h2>Concept Deep Dives</h2>
      <p><strong>Fair Value Gaps</strong> — search "ICT FVG explained" on YouTube. Look for videos that show how FVGs form, where they act as support/resistance, and how to tell if one is still "fresh" vs. filled.</p>
      <p><strong>Liquidity Sweeps</strong> — search "ICT liquidity sweep strategy". Understanding what makes a sweep valid (wick vs. body, close beyond) is essential before using NH/NL levels.</p>
      <p><strong>Market Structure</strong> — search "ICT market structure shift" or "ICT MSB". Knowing how to identify trend changes helps contextualize when an NH/NL sweep leads to a real reversal vs. continuation.</p>
      <p><strong>Inversion FVG (IFVG)</strong> — once you understand standard FVGs, the IFVG is the next step. Search "ICT IFVG entry model" for the specific entry trigger used in this strategy.</p>

      <h2>Trading Psychology</h2>
      <p><strong>"Trading in the Zone"</strong> by Mark Douglas — the classic on trading psychology. Covers how to think in probabilities, detach from individual trade outcomes, and build the mental framework needed for sweep-and-confirm models where not every setup wins.</p>
      <p><strong>"Best Loser Wins"</strong> by Tom Hougaard — short read on why accepting losses early actually improves your edge. Directly relevant to the sweep-and-confirm model where not every sweep works.</p>
      <p><strong>"Zen Trader"</strong> by Peter Castle — practical guide to emotional control in trading. Teaches how to stay calm during drawdowns, avoid revenge trading after a swept stop, and stick to the process.</p>
