      <h1>Philosophy</h1>
      <p class="lede">The foundation of this strategy is accepting what the market actually is, not what we wish it were.</p>
      <p>We cannot predict the future. Anything can happen at any moment — a sudden news event, an unexpected institutional order, a flash crash. The market is inherently unpredictable, and no amount of analysis changes that.</p>
      <p>This is why traders are not analysts. An analyst tries to forecast where the market will go. A trader does not need to. You do not need to know the direction. You do not need to know if a move is a retracement or a full reversal. You do not know when the retracement will end — and you never will.</p>
      <p>What you do know is this: the longer you stay in a trade, the more time you are exposed to risk. Every additional minute in a position increases the probability of being caught by something unpredictable. Time in the trade is exposure, and exposure is risk.</p>
      <p>This is why this strategy targets a small piece of the move — a slice of the retracement as price reverses from a swept level. You do not need to catch the whole move. You do not need to ride it to its end. You take what the reversal gives you, quickly, and you get out.</p>
      <p>Low risk-to-reward and high win rate is not a compromise. It is the only honest approach when you accept that the market is random, the future is unknown, and time is the enemy.</p>
