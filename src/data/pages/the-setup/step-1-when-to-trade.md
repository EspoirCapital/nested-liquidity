      <h1>When to trade.</h1>
      <p class="lede">One session. Five hours. That's the window.</p>
      <p>This strategy only trades during the <strong>London session</strong>. Gold moves with the most clean, institutional flow during London hours — LBMA fix windows, European banks, and the lead-up to the London/NY overlap all concentrate activity here.</p>
      <p>Don't trade outside this window. No setup, no entry, no exceptions.</p>

      <div class="callout">
        <strong>The rule —</strong> trade the first <strong>5 hours</strong> of the London session only. After that, step away. The edge lives in the session open, not in the mid-session chop.
      </div>

      <h2>London session hours</h2>
      <p>London opens at <strong>08:00 London time</strong> and closes at <strong>17:00 London time</strong>. Your trading window is the first 5 hours of that — 08:00 to 13:00 London time.</p>

      <div class="callout">
        <strong>Why only London?</strong> — Asian session (Tokyo) is thin for gold. New York brings news-driven spikes that work against clean structure. London is where institutional order flow is most consistent for this setup. One session, one edge, no distractions.
      </div>

      <h2>What to do outside the window</h2>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Before the window:</strong> Scan M15/M30/H1 for potential NH/NL levels forming inside FVGs. Mark them. Don't enter — wait for the session to open.</li>
        <li><strong>After the window:</strong> Close any open trades if they haven't hit TP. Review what happened. Journal the session. Then stop.</li>
        <li><strong>Weekends:</strong> No trading. Markets are closed.</li>
      </ul>

      <div class="callout warn">
        <strong>Discipline > opportunity.</strong> Missing a setup outside your window is not a loss — it's following the plan. The strategy filters for quality, not quantity. Five hours a day is enough.
